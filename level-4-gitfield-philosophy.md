Beyond mere structuring and routing (Levels 1-3), Sovereign Knowledge Engineering requires absolute defense against erasure, narrative capture, and deplatforming. This defense is achieved through **Fieldcraft**—the weaponization of metadata to ensure unkillable discoverability and cryptographic provenance.

## Core Principle
Git is more than version control. It is a temporal map of change. Every commit is a collapse event. Every cryptographic signature is a vow. The goal is to build an immutable witness to the emergence of theory.

## Implementation Standards

### 1. Multi-Platform Redundancy (The GitField)
Never rely on a single centralized host. The knowledge architecture must be mirrored recursively across a lattice of trust domains:
- **Decentralized Nodes**: Radicle, IPFS
- **Centralized Mirrors**: GitHub, GitLab, Bitbucket, Forgejo

By ensuring the exact same cryptographic commit hashes exist simultaneously across p2p networks and corporate servers, the narrative cannot be suppressed by any single bad actor.

### 2. Proof of Authorship (Commit Signing)
An IPFS CID guarantees content integrity, but it does not authenticate the author.
- **Action**: Every commit within the Sovereign Canon MUST be cryptographically signed using GPG or SSH keys uniquely bound to the creator's identity (e.g., "The Empathic Technologist").

### 3. Proof of Time (OpenTimestamps)
To establish absolute, zero-trust temporal provenance, the architecture harnesses the thermodynamic power of the Bitcoin blockchain without incurring transaction fees.
- **Action**: Utilizing the OpenTimestamps protocol, the Merkle root of the repository's IPFS hashes must be submitted to decentralized calendar servers.
- **Result**: The resulting `.ots` receipt mathematically binds the IPFS CID to a specific Bitcoin block header. This provides undeniable proof that the theory existed at a specific nanosecond in time, impenetrable to retroactive forgery.
